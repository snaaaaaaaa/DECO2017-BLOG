To begin the ideation process for our web app prototype, I first began by deconstructing the brief into its key factors, and evaluating what each may mean for the features and structure of the prototype.

<u>“Tailor made” hubs</u>

The page would have to have features and/or an aesthetic design representative of the chosen community. This could be in colours chosen, page layout, site structure, etc. In particular, the stand-out feature should be unique to the needs of the community. Moreover, “tailor made” can also include tailored advertising for the users in the community. 

<u>Advertising</u>

The chosen community should allow for the advertising of products. While this can apply to almost any hobby or community, some are better suited towards it than others.

<u>Chosen device platform</u>

Normally, such community apps are available on both mobile and desktop, with one primary platform. This would be informed by the chosen community - specifically, the user group the web app would target, as well as the content on the pages, and which screen size would better suit it. 

<u>The BlaBla Corp. social media network</u>
As users enter a universal login which they can use across multiple communities, this user flow should be integrated into the prototype to act as part of the larger BlaBla Corp. network. 

<u>Data</u>
The data which will be used and displayed in the prototype needs to be either easily sourced in likely large quantities, with variety amongst them. The data also needs to be open source and free from any copyright for ethical usage. 

With these factors in mind, I mapped a brainstorm of possible communities to centre our web app prototype around. 

[BRAINSTORM SKETCH]

From there, I narrowed it down to two key communities, which I felt had the most opportunities to fit the given brief, both conceptually and technically. Below is each community listed with the reasons I considered each one in relation to the brief, as well as prospective features. 


<u>Photography</u>

- I have both an interest in and knowledge about this community, allowing a first-hand perspective into user needs
- There are various aesthetic opportunities to create visually engaging pages with collages of photos 
- Large resource library of open source photos online to build app prototype 
- Can have individual profile pages to display the user’s photography as a collage and showcase their interests 
- Many advertising opportunities as camera parts and accessories are extensively available and sold 
- It is a community which can be divided / filtered into sub-communities (type of photography, medium, etc.)

<u>Home cooks</u>

- I have an interest and experience within this community as well. 
- A community not for professional chefs, but specifically for home cooks. This means it will may be featuring convenient, cheap recipes, rather than time-consuming ones which focus on indulgence or skill level
- Home cooks are a large, varied user base. The web app would have to accommodate people from a large variety of backgrounds, tech usage habits, etc. 
- Products to advertise are extensive (ingredients, utensils, dishes, pans, etc.) Could potentially link purchasing ingredients directly from recipe pages
- Opportunity to showcase a structured, dynamic filtering feature to filter recipes by cuisines, dietary needs, etc. 
- Large database of online recipes available to build pages for prototype 
Profile pages can showcase recipes posted by each user

Currently, the chosen community I am leaning towards is that of home cooks, due to the filtering options, large available database, and personal interest. Next lesson I will confer with my teammate and discuss each of our ideation processes to decide on a community.
